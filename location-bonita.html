Our Story

A corner built
for slowing down.

When we first drove through Midtown Bonita, we weren't looking for a location — we were looking for a feeling. A pocket of the neighborhood that felt unhurried, one where people actually stopped moving long enough to taste what was in their cup. We found it on the corner of Midtown Ave, in a storefront with high ceilings, morning light, and just enough room to do things right.

The design here is deliberate: warm plaster, reclaimed wood, and a bar low enough that you can watch every pour. Our sourcing relationships are just as considered — direct-trade lots from Colombia, Ethiopia, and Guatemala, roasted locally and adjusted for Florida's humidity. Every item on the menu is made here. Nothing is shipped in. This is the original Three Sixteen, and it still runs the way we intended it to.
