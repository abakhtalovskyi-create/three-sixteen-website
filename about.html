About / Three / Sixteen.

We started because we believed specialty coffee could be calm, accessible, and community-rooted — without being pretentious.

Three Sixteen is not a chain. It is not a concept. It is a commitment.

Founded 2021. Bonita Springs, FL.

How it started

It started with one rule: never compromise on quality.

Three Sixteen was founded in 2021 by Andrii B. in Bonita Springs, Florida. After years in hospitality and specialty coffee in Europe, the idea was simple: build the café that Southwest Florida was missing.

The name comes from the proportions used in specialty coffee — the standard brew ratio that produces a balanced, intentional cup. It became shorthand for everything we do: precise, deliberate, and never arbitrary.

We spent the first year visiting farms in Ethiopia, Colombia, and Guatemala before we opened. Not because we had to — because we believed what we served should be something we stood behind completely.

What we stand for

Intentional from farm to cup.

01
Intentional Craft

Every cup is a deliberate act — sourced from farms we know, roasted to bring out character, not to mask it. We don't do shortcuts.

02
Seasonal & Local

Our menu follows Florida's rhythm. What's in season stays on the menu. What's not, doesn't. No exceptions.

03
Community Space

Our cafes are designed to linger in — neighbourhood anchors built for connection, not just caffeine.

04
Radical Transparency

We share how we operate — costs, decisions, mistakes. Because trust is built in the open, not behind closed doors.

05
Quality Over Growth

We will never open a new location to meet a financial target. We open when we know we can maintain the standard.

06
People First

Our team are not gig workers. They have careers here. We invest in training, pay above market, and promote from within.

Our standards

What quality
means here.

Three standards. Non-negotiable. Every single day.

Coffee sourcing

We only buy from farms we've visited. We taste before we commit. We pay above Fair Trade — because Fair Trade is the minimum, not the standard.

In-house preparation

Everything baked, brewed, and prepared fresh on-site. We don't buy in and we don't cut corners. If it takes 36 hours to ferment properly, it takes 36 hours.

Team standards

Every barista who joins completes a minimum 4-week training programme. Standards are written down. They're enforced. Not because we're strict — because they matter.
